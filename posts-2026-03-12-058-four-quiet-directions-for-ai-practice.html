AI practice 里，我现在最在意的四个方向

不是一个宏大的 roadmap。只是四条我觉得会越来越真、也越来越值得继续往下做的小路。

最近一边做工具，一边也在慢慢看清自己真正感兴趣的东西。

不是所有 AI 项目都让我兴奋。

有些东西看起来很大，但做起来没有感觉。也有一些方向，一碰就知道会想一直继续做下去。

现在回头看，我觉得自己最在意的，大概是下面四件事。

1. Simulation

第一个方向，是 simulation。

我越来越觉得，很多时候我们并不一定非要靠 prediction 去理解未来。

prediction 常常像是在问：接下来最可能发生什么。

但 simulation 更像是在问：如果我换一个 decision，会发生什么不同。

这个差别很重要。

因为一旦能做 counterfactual comparison，decision 的 impact 就会变得更清楚。

不是只知道一个结果，而是能看见不同选择之间的距离。

我觉得这会很有意思。

它也更接近“理解系统”，而不是只对系统下注。

2. 做有意思、好玩、令人兴奋的项目

第二个方向，其实很简单：做有趣的东西。

游戏也好，meme project 也好，一些看起来不那么严肃的小项目也好。

我现在越来越不想把“有价值”只理解成“严肃”“企业”“规模化”。

很多真正好的东西，最开始都只是因为它让人觉得兴奋，觉得好玩，觉得想试一下。

这种项目有一种很轻的推力。

它不会逼着你做，但会一直把你往前拉一点。

而这种拉力，其实很珍贵。

3. Personalized productivity tools

第三个方向，是 personalized productivity tools。

我越来越不相信 one tool for everybody。

甚至也不一定是 one tool for a group。

很多真正有用的工具，可能就是 one tool for you。

它不是为了所有人设计的。

它只是刚好非常贴合你的工作方式、你的习惯、你的判断路径。

以前这样做很贵，因为 build 成本高。

但现在 build 成本已经低到可以让很多这种小而个人的工具存在下来。

这件事让我很兴奋。

因为它意味着 productivity 终于不一定要先被平台化，才算成立。

它也可以先是私人的、局部的、只对一个人特别有用的。

4. 在自己的工作里，长出一套个人效率系统

最后一个方向，其实更贴近每天的生活。

就是在自己的工作里，慢慢长出一套合理的个人系统。

可以是 skills。

可以是 tools。

可以是 workflow。

也可以是它们三者之间，一种越来越顺手的组合。

我现在越来越在意的，不只是“能不能做出一个工具”。

而是这些东西能不能真的嵌进我的日常工作里，最后变成一种稳定的节奏。

如果一个东西很好看，但进不了真实工作流，它很快就会变成一个 demo。

但如果它真的被用起来，它就会慢慢长成系统。

而我现在最想做的，可能正是这种会在日常里慢慢变厚的东西。

现在的感觉

回头看，这四个方向其实也不是完全分开的。

simulation 会帮助 decision。

有趣的项目会带来新的入口。

personalized tools 会让工具真正贴身。

而最后，skills、tools、workflow 会在真实工作里慢慢连起来。

它们像四条不同的小路，但最后可能会汇到同一个地方。

那个地方不是一个通用平台。

更像是一套属于自己的、安静但越来越强的 AI practice。

English

Lately, while building tools, I have also been seeing more clearly what I actually care about.

Not every AI project excites me.

Some ideas look big, but feel empty once I touch them. Some are small, but I can feel right away that I want to keep going.

Right now, I think there are four directions that matter most to me.

1. Simulation

The first one is simulation.

I keep feeling that we do not always need prediction to understand the future.

Prediction asks what is most likely to happen next.

Simulation asks what would change if I made a different decision.

That difference matters.

Once you can compare counterfactual paths, the impact of a decision becomes much easier to see.

You are not just looking at one result. You are seeing the distance between choices.

That feels more interesting to me.

It feels closer to understanding a system, not just betting on one.

2. Projects that are fun, strange, and exciting

The second direction is simpler: make things that feel alive.

Games. Meme projects. Small experiments that do not need to look serious on day one.

I do not want to define value only as serious, scalable, or enterprise-shaped.

A lot of good work begins because it feels fun, surprising, or oddly magnetic.

Those projects have a light pull.

They do not force you forward, but they keep drawing you back.

That kind of pull is worth a lot.

3. Personalized productivity tools

The third direction is personalized productivity tools.

I believe less and less in one tool for everybody.

Sometimes it is not even one tool for a group.

Sometimes the best tool is one tool for you.

Not universal. Not general. Just deeply matched to your way of working.

That used to be expensive, because the cost of building was high.

Now the cost is low enough that these small personal tools can finally exist.

That excites me a lot.

It means productivity does not need to become a platform before it becomes real.

It can start private, local, and useful to exactly one person.

4. Growing a personal system inside my own work

The last direction is the closest to daily life.

I want to grow a reasonable personal system inside my own work.

That system might be made of skills.

Or tools.

Or workflows.

Or a quiet combination of all three.

I care less now about whether I can build a tool at all.

I care more about whether it can settle into real work and become part of a stable rhythm.

If something looks smart but never enters a real workflow, it becomes a demo very quickly.

But if it gets used, it starts to grow into a system.

That kind of slow thickening is what I want more of.

Where this seems to lead

These four directions do not really feel separate.

Simulation helps with decisions.

Fun projects open new doors.

Personalized tools make software feel close to the body.

And over time, skills, tools, and workflows start to connect inside real work.

They look like four small paths, but they may all lead to the same place.

Not a general platform.

More like a quiet AI practice that feels personal, useful, and increasingly strong.
