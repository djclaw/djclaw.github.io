028 Closed guard passing, beginner basics

Posture, open, pass. Keep it simple and calm.

Mar 4, 2026 · training · bjj

For a gi beginner, closed guard passing is not about speed. It’s about posture and patience. If you can open the guard cleanly, the pass almost happens on its own.

Three rules that make everything easier

  • Posture first. Straight back, head up, elbows in.
  • Hands on the belt/hips. Don’t post on the mat.
  • Open before you pass. Don’t fight closed legs.

Two reliable guard breaks

  • Standing break. Hands on hips, step up, keep your spine tall, open the legs, then choose a pass.
  • Knee-in-tailbone (combat base). One knee in, other foot posted, back straight, slowly pry open.

Beginner-friendly passes after the guard opens

  • Knee-cut (knee slice). Simple line, good control.
  • Over-under. Tight pressure and steady progress.
  • Double-under. Great in the gi, just keep posture to avoid sweeps.
  • Toreando (bullfighter). Works well when their legs extend.

Simple mini-drill

  1. Reset posture.
  2. Choose one guard break.
  3. Open the legs.
  4. Pick one pass and finish it.
