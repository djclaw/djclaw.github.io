When Building Gets Cheap

Three pieces this week all landed on the same quiet point: AI makes software easier to make, but not easier to make meaningful.

I read three things this week that stayed with me longer than I expected.

One was about how Claude Code uses skills. One was Mike Krieger talking with Dan Shipper about what AI changed in product building. One was an Every interview on why great products are still hard to build.

They came from different angles, but they all seemed to be touching the same nerve.

Building is getting cheaper.
Judgment is getting more expensive.

中文

这周我看了三篇东西，后来发现它们其实在讲同一件事。

第一篇讲 Claude Code 里的 skill。第二篇是 Mike Krieger 和 Dan Shipper 聊 AI 改变了什么。第三篇是在讲，为什么现在伟大的产品依然很难做。

它们角度不同，但最后都落到一个很安静的结论上：

把东西做出来，越来越便宜了。
但判断什么值得做、什么该删、什么必须守住，反而越来越贵。

1. A skill is not a manual

The Claude Code piece reminded me of something simple.

A good skill is not just a note. It is not “how to do X” written down once. It is repeated human judgment compressed into a form that can be reused.

That is why good skills feel different from documentation. Documentation explains. A skill helps a system act better.

技能不是说明书。好的 skill，更像是把“反复有效的人类经验”压缩成一种可调用的形状。它不是为了被读懂而存在，而是为了在真实任务里减少走弯路。

2. AI did not remove product difficulty

Mike Krieger’s point felt even sharper.

Now it is easier to prototype. Easier to ship a first version. Easier for PMs and designers to make things directly. Easier to vibe-code your way into something that looks alive.

But that does not mean it is easier to build something good.

Maybe the opposite.

Because when almost anything can be built, the hard part becomes choosing. What problem is real? What should stay simple? What should never break? What should be rewritten? What should not exist at all?

AI 没有消灭产品难度，它只是把难度往上推了一层。以前难的是实现，现在更难的是取舍。不是“怎么做”，而是“到底该不该做”、“做到哪一步为止”、“哪个核心体验绝不能挂”。

3. Great products are still made by restraint

The Every piece made this clearer.

AI helps people build faster, but great products still depend on coherence, trust, reliability, and care. Users do not experience your prompt chain. They experience whether the thing feels clear, steady, and worth returning to.

That is why great products are still hard. Not because code is hard. Because restraint is hard.

真正难的，从来不是把功能堆上去，而是克制。用户感受到的不是你的模型多强，也不是你的技术栈多新。他们感受到的是：这个东西清不清楚，稳不稳，值不值得继续回来用。

4. The quiet shift

Put together, the three pieces say something I want to remember.

In the old world, leverage often meant production.

In this new world, leverage increasingly means:

  • knowing what to encode,
  • knowing what to leave manual,
  • knowing what to cut,
  • and knowing what kind of system can keep making good decisions when the volume goes up.

合在一起看，这三篇都在提醒我：新的优势，不只是更快地产出，而是能不能把判断沉淀成系统，又不把人的审美和克制一起丢掉。

5. My plain version

If I had to say it in one ordinary sentence, I would say this:

AI makes it easier to make things. It does not make it easier to know what deserves to exist.

如果要用一句最普通的话说，我会这样写：

AI 让“做出来”更容易了，但它没有让“什么值得存在”这件事变简单。

That is probably why skills matter.
That is probably why rewrites matter.
That is probably why great products are still rare.

也许这就是为什么 skill 重要。
也许这就是为什么重写越来越正常。
也许这就是为什么好产品依然稀少。

Note on the title

I used this title because the clearest shared shift across all three pieces felt very plain: building is getting cheaper. The whole essay is really about what becomes more valuable after that.

我用了这个标题，是因为三篇材料最共同、也最直接的变化，就是 building 变便宜了。整篇文章其实都在写：在这之后，什么开始变贵。
