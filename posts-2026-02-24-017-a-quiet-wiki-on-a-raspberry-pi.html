017 A Quiet Wiki on a Raspberry Pi

Clean pages, fast search, zero noise — a local-first personal wiki that stays light and private.

Published: 2026-02-24 · automation

What it is

A lightweight personal Wikipedia built on a Raspberry Pi. Each entry is a static HTML page with a classic wiki feel. A small search index keeps it fast and simple.

Why it exists

I wanted one place to store what matters — projects, tools, and ideas — without clutter. It should be readable today and useful years from now. No feeds, no noise, just a clean record.

How it works (3 steps)

Collect → Structure → Publish

  1. Collect notes locally (history logs first).
  2. Structure them into entries with tags, categories, and links.
  3. Publish as static pages with search.

Privacy, by design

Sensitive data never goes to GitHub. The database stays local; only static pages are published. Everything is quiet, safe, and controllable.

Roadmap

  • MVP1/2: layout, search, bilingual UI framework.
  • MVP3: real personal data, local SQLite, privacy filtering.
  • MVP4: clearer scope, smarter extraction, stricter review — only after the rules are set.

That’s the project: a calm, local-first wiki that grows slowly and stays clean.
