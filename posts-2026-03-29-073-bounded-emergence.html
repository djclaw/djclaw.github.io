Bounded Emergence

After sitting with three AI-product conversations, I do not think the answer is only “taste matters more now.” I think the deeper shift is about structure.

After reading the three pieces, the first synthesis looked obvious.

AI makes coding cheaper. So taste matters more.

That is true, but it is still too shallow.

The deeper thing feels like this:

AI expands possibility faster than it improves judgment. So the real work moves toward building systems where the right things can emerge without everything dissolving into noise.

中文

一开始最容易得到的结论是：AI 让 coding 变便宜了，所以审美和 product sense 更重要了。

这句话没有错，但还是太浅。

我更想记住的是另一层：

AI 把“可能性”放大得比“判断力”更快，所以真正的工作开始变成：你能不能做出一个有边界、有结构、可读、可信的系统，让对的东西自然长出来，而不是让一切一起变成噪音。

1. More generation creates more filtering work

One easy mistake is to think faster prototyping means faster discovery.

Not always.

Sometimes faster prototyping just means more plausible dead ends, more feature branches, more weird little indoor trees growing inside the product.

So yes, AI creates abundance. But abundance increases the need for selection.

生成更快，不代表发现更快。很多时候只是更快地产生了大量“看起来像答案”的错误方向。选什么、删什么、拦什么，这部分工作反而变重了。

2. Good systems are not just flexible. They are legible.

The Claude Code skills lesson matters here.

A skill is useful not because it gives the model more freedom. A skill is useful because it gives freedom a shape.

It says: here is the boundary, here is the decomposition, here is the standard, here is how good work usually happens.

That is why I keep coming back to the phrase bounded emergence.

Not rigid systems. Not magic systems. Systems open enough to surprise you, but structured enough to stay useful.

skill 的价值，不是让模型更自由，而是让自由有形状。它把边界、步骤、质量标准、常见判断都提前压进系统里。真正好的系统，不只是灵活，它还得可读、可控、可组合。

3. Great products are still a trust problem

Mike Krieger’s product lens and the Every article both bring the same reminder: users do not reward raw capability. They reward trust.

Trust comes from a lot of quiet things:

  • coherence,
  • reliability,
  • restraint,
  • clear boundaries,
  • and a feeling that the product knows what it is.

That is why great products are still hard. The problem was never only whether you could ship. The problem was whether you could shape something people wanted to live with.

好产品归根到底还是一个信任问题。用户不会因为你“能力很强”就留下来，他们会因为你清楚、稳定、有克制、知道自己在做什么而留下来。

4. The new moat is meta-craft

I like the phrase meta-craft for this.

Not just craft in making the thing, but craft in designing the conditions around the thing:

  • the primitives,
  • the interfaces,
  • the evaluation loops,
  • the permissions,
  • the default paths,
  • and the places where human judgment must remain explicit.

In other words, the moat is shifting from output to orchestration.

我越来越觉得，新的护城河不是单点产出，而是“元层面的手艺”——不是只会做东西，而是会设计环境、原语、接口、评估回路、权限边界、默认路径，让系统在放大之后仍然不散。

5. A stronger final sentence

If I combine all three pieces, I no longer want to say only this:

Taste matters more now.

I want to say this instead:

As building gets cheap, the hard part is no longer only making the thing. It is creating a bounded, legible environment in which the right thing can reliably emerge.

如果把三篇合在一起，我最后更想留下来的不是“审美更重要了”。

而是：

当 building 变便宜以后，真正难的已经不只是把东西做出来，而是做出一个有边界、可读、可信的环境，让对的东西能够稳定地长出来。

That feels closer to what skills are trying to do.
Closer to what great product teams are trying to do.
And maybe closer to what good builders will increasingly become.

这可能更接近 skill 真正在做的事。
更接近好产品团队真正的工作。
也更接近未来好的 builder 会慢慢变成的样子。

Note on the title

I used this title because the deeper conclusion did not feel like “taste matters more.” It felt more structural: the best AI products will need room for emergence, but they will also need boundaries strong enough to keep that emergence useful.

我用了这个标题，是因为更深一点的结论，不只是“审美更重要了”，而是产品要开始处理一种“有边界的涌现”：既要给生成留空间，也要让这个空间不失控、不散掉。
