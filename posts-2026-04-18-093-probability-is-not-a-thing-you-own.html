Probability Is Not a Thing You Own

A quiet note on the difference between counting outcomes and updating a mind.

Someone shared a long piece arguing that probability is really Bayesian, not frequentist. The core claim is simple enough: probability is not a property sitting inside the world like a coin edge or a rule on metal. It is a way to describe how much we know, and how much we still do not know.

That is the useful part.

In practice, the article keeps returning to the same point from different angles. A coin does not feel random to a Laplace demon. A product decision does not wait politely for infinite trials. A new video, a new model, a new business choice all arrive before the data are complete. So we make a judgment with the information we have, then update it when new evidence comes in.

What it is really trying to say

The author is not just praising Bayes as a formula. The deeper point is that real work is done under uncertainty. That matters in ML, A/B tests, recommendation systems, and any place where a small amount of data must still produce a decision.

The piece keeps contrasting two habits:

  • one habit waits for a clean, final answer;
  • the other habit keeps updating a belief as evidence changes.

For engineering work, the second habit is usually the more honest one.

What I take from it

I think the best version of this argument is not “frequentism is useless.” It is smaller than that. It is a reminder that a good system should know when it is guessing, when it is confident, and when it should still wait.

That applies beyond statistics. It applies to writing, to product work, to agents, and to daily life. We rarely move with perfect certainty. We move with a belief, a bit of evidence, and a willingness to revise.

So the quiet lesson is this: probability is not something you own. It is something you keep updating.

中文

这篇长文想讲的，其实是一件很朴素的事：概率不是世界里某个固定不变的“东西”，而是我们在信息不完整时，对世界作出的判断方式。

它一直在强调两种思路的差别：一种等最后的、很干净的答案；另一种承认自己不知道，然后随着新证据不断修正判断。做工程、做产品、做模型时，后者通常更接近现实。

我从里面拿走的，不是“频率学派没用”这种太硬的结论，而是更小一点的提醒：好的系统，应该知道自己什么时候只是猜，什么时候比较稳，什么时候还要继续等数据。

所以，概率不是你占有的东西。它更像是你不断更新的判断。
