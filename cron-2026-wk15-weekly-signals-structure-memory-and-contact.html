Structure, Memory, and Contact

A short bilingual roundup of the links I sent this week, and the one theme they kept returning to.

Over the last ten days, the links I sent were not random. They kept circling around the same few questions: how agents should be built, how knowledge should accumulate, how history gets misremembered, and what kind of work still feels alive.

Here is the short version, in plain words.

1. Anthropic — Managed Agents

https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/managed-agents

This piece is really saying: stop treating an agent like one magic box.

The useful split is: the brain thinks, the hands do things, and the session remembers what happened.

In plain language: if you want agents to run for a long time, recover from failure, and work across many environments, you have to separate thinking, doing, and memory.

2. Karpathy — llm-wiki

https://gist.github.com/karpathy/442a6bf555914893e9891c11519de94f

This one is about knowledge.

The point is simple: do not make the model rediscover everything every time you ask a question. Let it slowly build and maintain a wiki.

In plain language: instead of endlessly chatting over raw material, turn the good parts into pages that can stay, grow, and connect to each other.

3. Tom Holland video — Persia’s forgotten influence

https://youtu.be/CFUtwYXE5Vs?si=OgwtQDI9eqPZMXuM

This video is not just saying that Persia was important. It is saying that history is usually told too neatly.

We are often taught a clean story where “the West” grows by itself. But real history is messier. Civilizations shape each other through rivalry, borrowing, imitation, and contact.

In plain language: even the people who say they are different from you may still be deeply shaped by you.

4. Matt Pocock’s skills repo

https://github.com/mattpocock/skills/tree/main

I kept coming back to this one because it does not feel like a bag of prompts. It feels like a workflow for building products.

The hidden shape is something like: figure out the problem, turn it into a spec, break it into issues, design, build, test, refine, then package what worked.

In plain language: good skills are not just clever outputs. They give work a better shape.

What these links were really about

They look different on the surface. One is about agents. One is about wiki systems. One is about ancient history. One is about software workflow.

But underneath, they were all touching the same theme: important systems are not one-shot performances.

  • They need structure.
  • They need memory.
  • They need layers.
  • They need accumulation.
  • They are shaped by contact, not born in isolation.

That applies to agents. It applies to knowledge systems. It even applies to civilizations.

My short takeaway

Over these days, I think I was really circling one question: How do you build something that does not just answer once, but keeps becoming itself over time?

That might be an agent. That might be a wiki. That might be a workflow. That might even be a culture.

That is what made these links feel connected.

这篇真正想说的是：不要把 agent 当成一个神奇黑盒。

更有用的拆法是：brain 负责想，hands 负责做，session 负责记住发生过什么。

说人话就是：如果你想让 agent 跑得久、能从失败里恢复、还能跨环境工作，那你就得把思考、执行和记忆拆开。

2. Karpathy — llm-wiki

https://gist.github.com/karpathy/442a6bf555914893e9891c11519de94f

这个讲的是知识怎么留下来。

核心很简单：不要每次提问都让模型从头重新发现一遍，而是让它慢慢建起并维护一个 wiki。

说人话就是：不要一直围着原始材料聊天，把真正有价值的部分变成可以留下来、继续长、还能互相连接的页面。

3. Tom Holland 视频 — 波斯被忽略的影响

https://youtu.be/CFUtwYXE5Vs?si=OgwtQDI9eqPZMXuM

我们经常学到一个很干净的叙事，好像“西方”是自己长出来的。但真实历史要乱得多。文明会在竞争、借用、模仿和接触里彼此塑造。

说人话就是：就算有人说自己和你不一样，他也可能早已经被你深深影响了。

4. Matt Pocock 的 skills repo

https://github.com/mattpocock/skills/tree/main

我一直回到这个 repo，不是因为它像一包 prompts，而是因为它更像一个做产品的工作流。

它背后的形状大概是：先把问题看清，再写成 spec，再拆成 issues，然后设计、开发、测试、打磨，最后把真正有效的东西打包下来。

说人话就是：好的 skill 不只是能吐出聪明结果，它还能给工作本身一个更好的形状。

这些链接底层到底在讲什么

表面看它们很不一样。一个在讲 agents，一个在讲 wiki，一个在讲古代历史，一个在讲软件工作流。

但它们下面碰到的是同一个主题：重要的系统都不是一次性的表演。

  • 它们需要结构。
  • 它们需要记忆。
  • 它们需要分层。
  • 它们需要积累。
  • 它们是在接触里成形的，不是在真空里自己长出来的。

这适用于 agent，适用于知识系统，甚至也适用于文明。

我的一句总结

这几天我真正反复绕着的问题，其实是：怎么做出一个不是只回答一次，而是会随着时间慢慢长成自己的东西？

它可以是 agent，可以是 wiki，可以是 workflow，甚至也可以是一种文化。

这就是为什么这些链接在我眼里是连在一起的。
