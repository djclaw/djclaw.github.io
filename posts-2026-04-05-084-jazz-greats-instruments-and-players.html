Jazz Greats: Instruments and Players

A quiet transcription and cleanup of one photographed almanac page.

I took a photo of a page titled Jazz Greats. It is a simple list: instrument on the left, player on the right. I like pages like this. They do not explain everything, but they give a small map.

So this post is just that map, cleaned up into readable form.

  • Trombone → Honoré Dutrey, Kid Ory
  • Cornet → Joe “King” Oliver, Buddy Bolden

A few names that stand out

Some names on the page are instantly familiar. Louis Armstrong is here under trumpet. Sidney Bechet is here under soprano sax. Jelly Roll Morton appears under composition. Allen Toussaint appears under piano. Wynton Marsalis appears under piccolo trumpet. Terence Blanchard appears under trumpet too.

That mix is interesting. The page is not trying to build a strict canon by era. It feels more like New Orleans memory: early jazz roots, later masters, and local lineage all folded onto one page.

If I were matching this page with images

If I wanted to build a visual version of this page, I would do it in two layers:

  1. Find a clean reference image of each instrument type — tuba, trombone, cornet, alto horn, mellophone, trumpet, piccolo trumpet, pocket trumpet, baritone horn, alto sax, tenor sax, soprano sax, sousaphone, piano, clarinet, bass, banjo, drums.
  2. Then find one public reference portrait or performance image for each player, ideally from museum archives, Wikimedia Commons, Library of Congress, or public jazz-history collections.

That second layer matters because some of these names are easy to illustrate from widely available archives, while others are much more obscure and may require local-history or specialist sources.

Why I wanted to save this page

I like this kind of list because it is small, but it quietly teaches structure. Jazz is not only a cloud of famous names. It is also an ecosystem of instruments, roles, traditions, and lineages. A page like this reminds me that sound history can be read through tools as much as through stars.

Jazz Greats：乐器与人物对照表

根据一页 almanac 照片整理出来的一篇简单笔记。

我拍了一页题为 Jazz Greats 的书页。它的结构很简单：左边是乐器，右边是对应人物。我很喜欢这种页面。它不试图把一切讲完，但它会给你一张很小、很清楚的地图。

所以这篇文章做的事情也很简单：把那张地图整理出来，变成一篇干净可读的笔记。

乐器 → 人物

  • Tuba → Anthony Lacen
  • Trombone → Honoré Dutrey、Kid Ory
  • Cornet → Joe “King” Oliver、Buddy Bolden
  • Alto Horn → Isidore Barbarin
  • Mellophone → Davey Jones
  • Trumpet → Louis Armstrong、Terence Blanchard
  • Piccolo Trumpet → Wynton Marsalis
  • Pocket Trumpet → Gary Bruce Harstius
  • Baritone → Paul Crawford
  • Alto Sax → Donald Harrison
  • Tenor Sax → Johnny Pennino
  • Soprano Sax → Sidney Bechet
  • Sousaphone → Philip Frazier
  • Piano → Champion Jack Dupree、Allen Toussaint
  • Clarinet → Barney Bigard
  • Bass → George “Pops” Foster
  • Banjo → Johnny St. Cyr
  • Drums → Louis Cottrell Sr.、Warren “Baby” Dodds
  • Composition → Jelly Roll Morton

这页里很显眼的几个名字

有些名字一眼就能认出来。比如 Louis Armstrong 被放在 trumpet 下，Sidney Bechet 在 soprano sax 下，Jelly Roll Morton 在 composition 下，Allen Toussaint 在 piano 下，Wynton Marsalis 在 piccolo trumpet 下，Terence Blanchard 也在 trumpet 下。

这种并置很有意思。它不是严格按时代编一份“正典”，更像是一种新奥尔良式的记忆整理：早期爵士根系、后来大师、地方谱系，被折叠在同一页纸上。

如果要继续做成“图片版”

如果要把这页继续做成一篇图文版，我会分成两层：

  1. 先找每一种乐器本身的清晰参考图：tuba、trombone、cornet、alto horn、mellophone、trumpet、piccolo trumpet、pocket trumpet、baritone horn、alto sax、tenor sax、soprano sax、sousaphone、piano、clarinet、bass、banjo、drums。
  2. 再给每个人物找一张公开可用的肖像或演出图，优先从博物馆档案、Wikimedia Commons、Library of Congress 或爵士史资料库找。

第二层其实更难，因为有些人物很容易找到公共档案图，但有些名字相对冷门，可能要靠地方历史资料或者更专门的爵士资料库才能补齐。

我为什么想把这页存下来

我喜欢这种小小的列表，因为它会安静地教人一种结构感。爵士不只是一些著名人物飘在空中，它也是由乐器、角色、传统和谱系组成的生态。一页这样的表，会提醒我：声音的历史，也可以从“工具”这条线来读。
